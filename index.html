Our Company

A Legacy Built on Energy

Founded in 1996 and headquartered at the Jollibee Center Building in Ortigas, Pasig City, Filpride Energy Corp. (FEC) is a trusted name in the Philippine downstream oil industry. As a proud member of the Villavicencio Group of Companies, FEC is engaged in the wholesale and retail of petroleum products — including light oil and kerosene — serving construction companies, transportation fleets, and the general motoring public.

Operating under the USA88 retail brand, FEC has established a network of service stations primarily across Metro Manila and nearby provinces, with plans for continued expansion. The company also undertakes real estate leasing operations, further diversifying its business portfolio.

Through its sister company Filpride Resources, Inc., the group handles the importation, supply, and storage of petroleum products, creating a vertically integrated supply chain that ensures quality and reliability from port to pump.

Retail Network

The USA88 Brand

USA88 is Filpride Energy Corp.'s consumer-facing retail fuel brand — a network of modern, full-service gasoline stations offering quality fuels to motorists across Metro Manila and nearby provinces.

The brand was launched as part of FEC's strategic entry into the retail fuel market, complementing its wholesale and B2B operations. Under CEO Mary Ann Villavicencio-Sy, USA88 was programmed to expand to 20+ new outlets.

Stations are positioned in high-traffic corridors across Metro Manila, Rizal, and nearby provinces. The brand also participated in the Green Energy E-Transport Project in partnership with TotalEnergies and Basic Energy Corp., making select USA88 stations solar-powered hubs.

As part of the broader Villavicencio Group ecosystem — which includes Filoil, Flying V, and PetroGazz — USA88 benefits from shared logistics, supply chain synergies, and the group's collective purchasing power.

Market Position

Industry Standing

Filpride Energy Corp. is a recognized independent oil player in the Philippine petroleum industry — competing in the "minor oil players" segment alongside Seaoil, Phoenix, Unioil, Jetti, and Eastern Petroleum, as distinct from the dominant "Big Three" of Petron, Shell Pilipinas, and Caltex.

CEO Mary Ann Villavicencio-Sy served as Vice President of the Independent Philippine Petroleum Companies Association (IPPCA), representing the sector in advocacy work including PUV fuel discount programs, anti-illegal retailing campaigns, and industry self-regulation.

The Philippine downstream oil market was deregulated under Republic Act 8479 (Oil Deregulation Law) in 1998, allowing independent players like Filpride to enter and compete freely. Filpride also participates in the industry's synchronized fuel price adjustment mechanism alongside Petron, Shell, Caltex, and other major players.

Sustainability

Green Energy Commitment

Filpride Energy Corp., under the USA88 brand, is actively contributing to the Philippines' clean energy transition. As part of the Green Energy E-Transport Project, the company is collaborating with strategic partners to reduce carbon footprint while evolving to serve the transportation needs of the future.

The initiative involves solarization of USA88 retail fuel stations — converting them into hybrid energy hubs that generate clean solar power while dispensing conventional fuels. Complementing this, the project integrates electric buses (e-buses) into the public transport ecosystem.

These efforts position Filpride at the forefront of the Philippine independent oil sector's transition toward a low-carbon, multi-energy economy.
